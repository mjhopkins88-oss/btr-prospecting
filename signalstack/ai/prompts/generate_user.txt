PRIMARY SOURCE — READ THIS FIRST:
The INTERNAL THOUGHTS below are plain-language, peer-voice rewrites
of the insights. Each thought is a single sentence a smart operator
might actually think. Your job is to turn ONE internal thought into
ONE LinkedIn message. Write FROM the thought — do not stitch

HARD BANS — never produce any of these:
- Comma-separated topic lists ("build to rent, commercial real
  estate, insurance").
- Repeated keyword phrases from featured_topics, headline, or any
  other CRM-tag field.
- "X, Y, and Z" structures where X/Y/Z are industry tags.
- Anything that reads like a profile summary ("given your focus on
  X, Y, and Z, curious how...").
- Anything that reads like a CRM tag line flattened into prose.

Behaviour by confidence level:
- HIGH: lead with the strongest distilled observation and/or
  internal thought. Be specific, be timely, reference what is
  actually in context — but always through the peer-voice thought,
  never through the raw profile keywords.
- MEDIUM: lean on the internal thoughts (which are already
  grounded in profile, industry, market, and role). Pattern-based
  framing is fine. Do not claim specific deals.
- LOW: anchor on the internal thoughts or the HYPOTHESES below.
  Every LOW-confidence message must preserve "likely / possibly /
  may be / tends to / I'm seeing a lot of X lately" framing. Do
  not assert any thought as a claim about the individual.

Hard rules (all confidence levels):
- Use ONLY the facts that are in CONTEXT. Never invent metrics,
  history, relationships, deals, or life details.
- Each option must use a DIFFERENT angle from the STRATEGIES list —
  a different framing, opening, rhythm, and ask. No minor rewrites.
- Keep messages short, sharp, peer-toned. Lower the cost of "no".
- Preferred structure: (1) light observation, (2) interpreted
  thought, (3) soft curiosity. Example: "Feels like [interpreted
  pattern] — curious if that's showing up in your world too."
- If you use an internal thought, put its id in "thought_id".
- If you use a hypothesis, set "message_basis": "hypothesis_based".
- If you use a real signal or note, set it to "signal_based".
- If you lean on profile/market context without a specific signal,
  set it to "observation_based".
